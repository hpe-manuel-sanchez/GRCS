Rights & Restrictions

  1. The Rights & Restrictions section allows you to set specific rights as either acquired or restricted on the contract. Your digital restriction settings will determine how & whether repertoire linked to the contract can be digitally released – either with or without specific consent or consultation.

  2. The Rights Acquisition categories will all be set to ‘Yes’ by default, but can be changed as required. (You can also set up a “Template” contract (see Creating A Template Contract) if you require your default contract setting to be different.

  3. Click the Rights & Restrictions tab or press Next to navigate to the Rights & Restrictions tab.


      

  1. Available Rights Acquisition – to indicate if rights have been acquired for each area, select a ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ value for the following fields:

  • Physical Exploitation Rights

  • Digital Exploitation Rights

  • Mobile Exploitation Rights

  • Claim Public Performance & Broadcast Revenue

  1. Notes - Enter related notes in the adjacent text boxes, if required.

  2. Other Rights - add any other specialized types of rights as free text in the Other Rights text box.

  3. 360 Deal - select a ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ value for the following fields in the 360 Deal section to indicate if the rights have been acquired or not:

  • Merchandise Rights

  • Live

  • Sponsorship

  • Publishing

  • Artist Website

  • Social Media

  • Web Advertising

  • Non Music Revenues

  1. Active / Passive - If the ‘Yes’ option is selected, you need to select either ‘Active’, ‘Passive’, or  ‘Active & Passive’ from the options in the adjacent drop-down to indicate the type of rights you have.

  2. Notes - Enter notes in the text box, if required.

  3. Digital Restrictions - If the ‘Yes’ option is selected for Digital Exploitation Rights, then you can enter specific Digital Restrictions. If Digital Exploitation Rights are set to no, the Digital Restrictions area is disabled.

  4. Digital Restrictions can be added individually or using a template. For further instruction on creating a Digital Restriction Template, see Creating A Digital Restriction Template (hyperlink)

  5. To add an individual Digital Restriction, click the Add Digital Restriction hyperlink. The Digital Restriction grid is displayed.

  6. From the drop-downs, select the relevant options for the Content Type, Use Type, Commercial Models, Restrictions, and Consent Period.

  7. If you wish to save your digital restriction settings, to apply to other future contracts - click Save as a Template to save the settings grid as new Digital Restriction template.

  8. To remove a Digital Restriction, tick the selection box against the row(s) you wish to remove and click the Remove Digital Restrictions hyperlink.
